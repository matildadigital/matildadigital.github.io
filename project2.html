Aerospace Alliance App

The Aerospace Alliance serves to establish the Southeast region as a world-class aerospace and aviation corridor. Business leaders interested in working with Alliance companies needed to easily understand them and their products and services. I designed this app to replace the bulky catalogs that previously filled this need.

The Problem

Business leaders need information about the aerospace and defense companies across the Gulf state region so they can select vendors for manufacturing and service contracts. The typical medium for this information was a bulky, burdensome catalog with static information. Business leaders needed a portable way to stay current with capabilities across the region.

Research: Persona

Product Development Paula

Age: 54

Goals: Sharing target vendors with her product development team. Staying abreast of new offerings or new organizations in the region.

Pain Points: Packing vendor catalogs in suitcases. Keeping up with trade organization publications.

Methods: Wireframing

The sharing feature with colleagues about space and defense vendors would make it easier for business leaders to decide which companies to contract with. The news updates help business developers stay abreast of new capabilities and offerings in the region to facilitate planning for product and project management. Organizing vendors by name and location makes finding their information easier and seeing which organizations are local.

Methods: Interface Design

Our users we busy, on-the-go business professionals who wanted updates on the region's suppliers and manufacturers. They needed a user interface that made information easy to read and navigate. The design emphasized the most relevant information for their decision making needs. In this example of the regional news feed, users are given the news headline, the date and time, the state the story originated from, and the logo of the featured supplier or manufacturer. Remaining details are saved for the article detail screen.

Looking Forward

The app was developed with a adminstrative portal for managing content, e.g. adding news articles and changing supplier information. In the next release, the app would be better as a platform where individual suppliers and manufacturers would maintain news and content for their own listing, similar to social media equivalents like Facebook's Pages. While this would add the need for content governance to managing the app, it would relieve the organization of content creation and management while preserving the app's value.
