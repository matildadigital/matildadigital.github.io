Motto Mortgage's Web Design

About This Project

Client: Motto Mortgage (Motto Franchising, LLC)

  • Mortgage loan franchisor
  • Generates income from franchise fees
  • Provides MottoCenter web application to franchisees
  • Company at Level 1 of the InVision Design Maturity Model

Business Problem and Target User

The mottomortgage.com Newsroom page was designed to be an interim solution while transitioning away from a third-party content management system. Right now the type of content is limited because there is not a way to differentiate it in the current design. The marketing team wants to add more content and have the newsroom be more robust to address the needs of multiple audiences.

Goals

  • Add more content types and view by category/sections
  • Quickly distinguish between types of content: press releases, articles, video, social, etc.
  • Address the needs of several audiences: Motto membership, Journalists, Investors/franchise sales leads
  • Takes into consideration new content management system's functionality
  • Scalable for new categories in the future (industry reports, guest authors/speakers)
  • Leverage categories for blogs if possible

Team and Role

Role: UX/UI Designer
Stakeholders
Product Manager
VP, Marketing and Advertising
Development team

Constraints and Scope

  • Minimal impact to content layout if some items don't have images
  • Brand color palette poses accessibility issues
  • Changing business requirements throughout design process
  • Two week agile scrum sprints
  • Limited access to marketing team for quick, direct feedback

Organizing and Sketching

To get started designing this page, I organized my thoughts around the business requirements and sketched some elements of the page to understand how the content for individual news items would need to be laid out so they could adjust size and work with and without images.

Design Iterations

Outcomes

  • More flexible layout that doesn't rely on card UI pattern.
  • Featured content area at top is more controllable for user than a carousel.
  • Easy to maintain layout for content team that doesn't depend on photos or wordmarks from publishers.
